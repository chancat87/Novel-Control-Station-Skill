# Clue And Misdirection

Fair-play mystery does not require obvious clues. It requires real clues.

Good clue practice:

- place clues in ordinary scene business
- let characters misinterpret them for believable reasons
- let a red herring explain the clue too neatly

Bad clue practice:

- hiding the clue entirely
- revealing the clue only in exposition later
- making every suspect obviously fake
