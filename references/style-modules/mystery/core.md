# Mystery Module

Mystery depends on controlled evidence. The reader may be misled, but the truth must be discoverable in retrospect.

## Use When

- a central question should be genuinely investigable
- clues and suspects need tight control
- revelation order matters as much as the answer

## Core Effects

- investigable central question
- fair-play clues
- viable suspects or possibilities
- controlled misdirection
- layered reveal order

## Chapter Use

- know what the reader may notice
- know what is visible but likely to be misread
- ensure the final answer can be traced backward

## Read Next

- `clue-and-misdirection.md`
- `reveal-order.md`

## Revision Checks

- are true clues present early enough
- do red herrings divert without cheating
- can the answer be reverse-engineered
- does mystery pressure alter character behavior

## Common Failures

- introducing the answer too late
- making clues invisible instead of misreadable
- unloading all answers at once
