# Humor Module

Use humor as story force, not floating joke delivery. The strongest comic chapters make pressure visible through embarrassment, bias, status damage, or callback payoff.

## Use When

- a chapter needs release without losing stakes
- character friction should become funny before it becomes costly
- dialogue needs sharper timing

## Core Effects

- tension release through character behavior
- status reversal
- setup and payoff
- escalation
- callback
- compressed dialogue rhythm

## Chapter Use

- define who becomes funniest under pressure and why
- anchor humor to desire, fear, shame, or imbalance
- keep setups short and payoffs clearer than the setup
- let callbacks reveal relationship or character truth

## Revision Checks

- does humor grow from character rather than hover above the scene
- is the setup shorter than the burden it creates
- do callbacks evolve instead of merely repeating
- does humor serve the chapter mission

## Common Failures

- writing stand-up instead of fiction
- turning everyone into the same joke machine
- sacrificing emotional continuity for a cheap gag
