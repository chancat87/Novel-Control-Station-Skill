# Horror Module

Horror works by making the familiar feel wrong and progressively harder to survive or explain. Fear should move through perception, implication, and consequence.

## Use When

- dread should intensify across the chapter
- ordinary reality needs destabilization
- perspective limits should sharpen disturbance

## Core Effects

- dread
- distorted normality
- sensory disturbance
- limited perspective
- escalating violation
- lingering aftereffect

## Chapter Use

- decide what ordinary thing becomes wrong
- decide what the point of view can and cannot know
- make fear concrete through sensory evidence and consequence

## Revision Checks

- does the scene disturb before it explains
- is fear sensory rather than abstract
- does the limited perspective deepen dread
- does the chapter leave residue after the scare

## Common Failures

- overexplaining too early
- describing fear without evidence
- noise instead of dread
