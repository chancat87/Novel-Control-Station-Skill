# Fantasy Module

Fantasy is strongest when the world feels inhabited rather than merely explained. Strange systems need rules, cost, and everyday texture.

## Use When

- invented rules or powers matter in the chapter
- social texture should show the world is lived in
- world information must stay coherent without dumping lore

## Core Effects

- coherent rules
- visible costs
- social and institutional texture
- sense of history
- everyday lived reality
- controlled information release

## Chapter Use

- define which world rule matters now
- show it through use, friction, or consequence
- add at least one lived-in detail when texture matters

## Revision Checks

- are relevant rules consistent
- is cost visible where power appears
- does the world feel inhabited beyond the main cast
- does the strange element change behavior or society

## Common Failures

- lore dumping
- power without cost
- empty world outside the main cast
- changing rules for convenience
