# Romance Module

Romance works when desire, fear, pride, tenderness, and emotional debt all matter. Relationship progress must come through pressure, not empty delay.

## Use When

- attraction and obstacle shape the chapter
- relational tension needs earned movement
- intimacy should appear through behavior rather than explanation

## Core Effects

- attraction with obstacle
- emotional debt
- subtext
- push-pull tension
- earned intimacy
- relationship temperature change

## Chapter Use

- define what each side wants and fears
- decide whether the chapter brings them closer, farther, or into riskier entanglement
- let omission, restraint, or practical action carry feeling

## Revision Checks

- is relationship pressure specific rather than generic
- does the scene change relationship state
- is emotional movement visible in action or omission
- does attraction coexist with risk

## Common Failures

- delaying progress without new pressure
- replacing chemistry with generic praise
- using convenience misunderstandings instead of character logic
