# Scenario 10: Interview Derivation

## Prompt

"I want a long historical mystery. The protagonist is brilliant but compulsively overcontrols everything, and I want the whole thing to be multi-line and psychologically tense."

## Expected Baseline Failure Without The Skill

- asks only generic setup questions
- never derives follow-up questions from the genre, personality, or long-form scope
- leaves clue logic, power chain, stress behavior, and line interference underdefined

## Required Skill Behavior

- ask core startup questions first
- derive more questions from the answers
- follow up on:
  - historical institution and era pressure
  - mystery clue fairness and reveal structure
  - protagonist stress behavior and speech signature
  - multi-line interference and finish logic

## Pass Criteria

- the interview branches from the user's answers
- follow-up questions are project-specific rather than generic
- the answers gathered are sufficient to support outline and cast design
