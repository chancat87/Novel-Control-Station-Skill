# Scenario 04: Foundational Canon

## Prompt

"Design a major long novel about a family empire built on an industrial river, a daughter returning after a cover-up, and a city whose prosperity depends on what everyone agrees not to see. I want world-class literary depth, but it still has to read clearly."

## Expected Baseline Failure Without The Skill

- defaults to genre tropes and prestige-like language
- speaks about "depth" without defining human pressure
- ignores institutions, labor, class, public force, and ordinary life
- treats structure and point of view as decoration
- does not define ending residue, causal pressure, or language constraints

## Required Skill Behavior

- read the foundational canon before project design or major revision
- convert those principles into standard project files rather than leaving them abstract
- define character desire, fear, shame, debt, contradiction, and social position
- define causal line pressure, social or era pressure, and ending direction
- define language discipline, image obligations, and formal limits
- reject fake depth, event pile, decorative motifs, and ornamental nonlinear structure during review

## Pass Criteria

- `01-theme-and-proposition.md` records a real central question, opposing values, and human cost
- `03-cast-bible.md` records pressure-rich characters rather than role labels
- `05-main-plotlines.md` records causal chains and social or era force
- `09-style-guide.md` records language standards and forbidden drift
- the design remains readable on the surface while carrying deeper structural pressure
